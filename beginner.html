Select the purpose of your satellite

What is the purpose of your satellite?

Satellite telephone system

Allows people to talk over the phone, even from remote regions of the world. The satellite receives signals from the ground and transmits them to other areas on Earth.

Budget:100 credits

Satellite internet

Allows people to connect to the internet, even from remote regions of the world. The satellite receives signals from the ground and transmits them to other areas on Earth.

Budget:200 credits

Weather forecasting

Monitor weather systems so forecasters can predict future weather. Weather satellites need to be able to take images of clouds and use thermal cameras to measure the temperature of the ground and oceans.

Budget:200 credits

Natural disaster monitoring

When natural disasters occur, satellites are used to assess the damage. High resolution images are needed to see details of the damage on Earth, so satellites must orbit close to the ground. Infrared cameras can show floods and fires. Radio waves can show regions where the earth has been warped by earthquakes.

Budget:500 credits

Satellite navigation system

Uses signals from four separate satellites so sat nav users can find their location on Earth. These satellites need to be able to see huge area of Earth at a time, measure time accurately and send information about their location.

Budget:100 credits

Map-making

Map the surface of the Earth. Your satellite needs to be able to take images of small areas of the Earth's surface and measure the height of the ground in detail.

Budget:200 credits

              Select the instruments for your satellite

              To complete your mission you will need the correct instruments. You only have a limited number of slots so choose wisely!

              Requirements

              Available instruments:

              • Atomic clock
                1 credits
                A very accurate clock used by navigation satellites to measure the time very precisely. They are used to help people find their locations on Earth.
              • Thermal camera
                30 credits
                Provides thermal images of Earth's atmosphere. This camera is useful for forecasting the weather, even during hours of darkness.
              • Camera
                10 credits
                Takes pictures of the Earth that can be used to create maps or track natural disasters, such as floods or volcanic eruptions. This camera can also be used to take pictures of clouds.
              • Radio dish
                1 credits
                Can be used to control up to 4 instruments. It can send images and data from these 4 instruments back to Earth.
              • GPS receiver
                2 credits
                Used to communicate with other GPS satellites.
              • Internet encoder
                0 credits
                Used to broadcast the internet to people around the world.
              • Phone encoder
                1 credits
                Broadcasts signals between telephones across the world.
              • Radar altimeter
                5 credits
                Used to measure the shape of the ground, including mountains and valleys on Earth's surface. This instrument also provides information about the effect of earthquakes. It requires a radar dish to work.
              • Radar dish
                1 credits
                Sends and receives signals from the satellite to Earth.
              • Radio transmitter
                1 credits
                Sends information collected by the satellite to Earth.
